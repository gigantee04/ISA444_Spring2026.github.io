# ISA444_Spring2026.github.io
Across all tested methods, LightGBM proved to be the best forecasting model for this hotel dataset, producing the lowest error rates (MAE: 0.081) and minimal bias by accurately picking up on complex booking patterns using outside data such as pricing, holidays, and events. In terms of overall performance, LightGBM ranked first, followed by NBEATS as a close second due to its ability to handle seasonal trends, TimesFM-2.5 as the top model that required no prior training on the data, and AutoARIMA with predictors as a solid step up from basic statistical methods. One of the most important findings was that adding outside variables, like pricing and event data, was the biggest factor in improving forecast accuracy, which was clear from the large performance gap between standard AutoARIMA and the versions that included those extra inputs. Although Google's TimesFM-2.5 performed well without needing any local training data, it still could not beat a model trained directly on this hotel's own historical data, showing that for hotels with a long record of data, a locally trained model like LightGBM remains the best option. For real-world use, LightGBM is the recommended go-to model. However, for new hotels that do not yet have enough historical data to train a model, TimesFM-2.5 is the best temporary option until enough data has been collected.
